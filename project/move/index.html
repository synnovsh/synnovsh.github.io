Background

In the summer of 2019 I interned at Iterate, and worked with the food start-up Dagens.

Dagens connects food producers, like farmers, to restaurants and grocers. Farmers can post interesting produce and sell directly to consumers, and Dagens provides the platform and handles logistics.

The team was tasked to evaluate how we could support the transportation of parcels with a new information system. How can a physical parcel be traced? What information does the logistics-partner need? Parcels can be collected from several farms, go through different intermediary stops, and be distributed to several points. The resulting system should represent relevant information at each step in a meaningful way.

The interns started by visiting the farms, and hopped on the van to distribute across Oslo-city. We witnessed missing deliveries, when there weren't enough cucumbers, and were in the van when eggs cracked under transportation. We met passionate farmers who aimed for sustainability and wanted innovation, and worked with a logistics partner transitioning from paper to digital logging.

1/3

Results

The resulting system presents information related to parcels and orders to the relevant logistics partner, from each step at their journey -- "farm to table". A React webapp was developed to fetch and post data to Dagen’s existing Sanity.io CMS, organising and represent the content in a meaningful way.
